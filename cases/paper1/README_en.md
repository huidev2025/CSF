# Case Study: CSF Paper Writing Process

[简体中文](README.md) &#124; **English**

> A real-world record of CSF in **academic writing**. 12 dialogue logs + bilingual arXiv submissions, documenting the full collaboration from "discussing the skeleton" to "arXiv submission."

---

## Background

**"This Paper Was Written by AI"** is an academic paper targeting ICSE 2027 IOR (Ideas, Visions, and Reflections) track. The subject of the paper is CSF itself — **semantic drift in long-range human-AI collaboration** — introducing two core concepts: "Index Sickness" and the "Pang Principle."

The writing process itself is a **live demonstration** of the paper's thesis: all text was generated by AI collaborators, with the Owner providing only directional judgment. The engineering experience accumulated across 391 CSF sessions was used to manage the semantic space during paper writing.

## CSF Mechanisms at Work

**1. Multi-Agent Collaboration**  
Multiple AI models and personas were used across different stages. The Owner switched agents based on the task. Each agent reconstructed project state from context at session start.

**2. Purpose-Driven Writing**  
Not "outline first, then fill in." Each session had a clear single purpose — collect material, or revise structure, or polish language. No mixing.

**3. Physical Separation (Baseline-Log Separation)**  
The manuscript files and dlog dialogue logs are strictly separated. The manuscript holds only conclusions; the logs hold the complete process. This is the mechanism the paper itself advocates, instantiated in the writing process.

**4. Adversarial Review (Simulated Harsh Reviewer)**  
In dlog_10, the AI was asked to role-play "the most苛刻 (harsh and seasoned) reviewer," producing 14 criticisms. Owner and AI then responded point by point — 7 accepted for revision, 6 rebutted, 1 left to Owner discretion. This is CSF's "AI aliveness" in action: the AI judged independently based on logic, not Owner preference.

## On dlog File Readability

The raw dialogue logs retain **Copilot tool invocation traces** (`Read`, `Searched`, `Created` lines) and some AI intermediate reasoning output. These are uncleaned because they are themselves **verifiable evidence** — a third party can verify whether cases and arguments cited in the paper indeed originate from the corresponding session records.

If you just want to understand the process, this page is enough. If you want to verify the evidence, dig into the dlog raw files.
